About

Sakshi Sharma, M.Sc. Bioinformatics
Computational Biology Researcher

Hi, I’m Sakshi Sharma, an Indian computational biologist working at the intersection of biology and artificial intelligence. My work focuses on building machine learning models to understand disease progression and support better clinical decision-making. What drives me in this field is the idea that when interpreted thoughtfully, biological data can change how we understand and treat disease.

My journey into this field was not linear. I began with a strong foundation in biology, aspiring to pursue medicine. Over time, my curiosity shifted toward understanding the underlying mechanisms of disease and how data could be used to study them. This transition led me to bioinformatics, where I discovered the potential of combining computational methods with biological insight to solve complex problems.

During my postgraduate studies in Bioinformatics at Jamia Millia Islamia, I worked on projects involving transcriptomic data analysis, RNA-seq pipelines, and machine learning applications in biomedical contexts. Through these experiences, I gained a deeper understanding of both the technical and biological aspects of data-driven research.

I'm keen to advance my work in deep learning, multi-omics integration, and single-cell data analysis. I hope to build models that are not just technically strong but also useful in real clinical settings. For me, it’s important that my work contributes to healthcare that is not only advanced but also more accessible—leveraging computational approaches to enable early diagnosis, better treatment strategies, and equitable care.

Beyond technical skills, I consider my strongest qualities to be curiosity, adaptability, and the ability to learn deeply. More than anything, I try to stay curious, keep learning, and approach things with an open mind.

Outside of research, I have a deep appreciation for fiction—both in literature and movies. I love doing Yoga. Yoga has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember; it’s something I genuinely enjoy and return to, much like a favorite routine. I also love cooking my own meals, especially keeping them simple, healthy, and something I can enjoy creating.
